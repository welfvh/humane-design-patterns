A Pattern Language for Respectful Technology

Design patterns that respect
attention, autonomy, and wellbeing

Technology shapes behavior. These 42 patterns describe how to build products and interfaces that serve people rather than exploit them -- the bright inverse of dark patterns.

Seven Principles

The foundations that every humane design pattern builds upon

Respectful

Prioritize people's time, attention, and digital wellbeing. Match delivery to urgency. Adapt to context.

Empowering

Put people in control of their experience. Enable management of algorithms, privacy, and usage patterns.

Finite

Set boundaries on engagement. Signal when content ends. Replace infinite with intentional.

Transparent

Be clear about intentions, honest in actions, and free of dark patterns. Show what data is collected and why.

Intentional

Employ friction to prevent misuse. Direct people toward healthier digital behaviors. Prioritize lasting over immediate.

Inclusive

Design for the full range of human diversity. Build for disabilities first. Give users control over how they interact.

Resilient

Prioritize wellbeing of vulnerable users. Anticipate misuse. Design for edge cases, not just the happy path.

Attention Respectful

Finite Feed

Replace infinite scroll with content that has a clear end. Show an "all caught up" indicator when users reach the latest content, giving them a natural exit point.

Inverts Infinite scroll, bottomless feeds
Used by Instagram (partially), LinkedIn, Are.na
Attention Finite

Manual Load

Require explicit user action to load more content. Replace auto-loading with a deliberate "Load More" button that preserves the user's sense of where they are.

Inverts Auto-refresh, pre-fetching, endless pagination
Used by Hacker News, many RSS readers
Attention Respectful

Conscious Autoplay

Never auto-play media without explicit consent. Require a deliberate user action before video or audio begins. Respect that attention is not yours to take.

Inverts Auto-playing videos, auto-advancing episodes
Used by Safari (default), Firefox, most accessibility tools
Attention Empowering

Demetrication

Remove or hide vanity metrics (likes, follower counts, view numbers) that create social comparison anxiety and compulsive checking. Let content stand on its own merit.

Inverts Like counts, follower displays, viral metrics
Used by Instagram (optional), Demetricator extension, Are.na
Attention Respectful

Aesthetic Dampening

Reduce visual stimulation to lower dopaminergic pull. Use grayscale modes, muted thumbnails, and subdued color palettes to make interfaces less compulsive.

Inverts Bright notification badges, eye-catching thumbnails, red counters
Used by iOS grayscale mode, DF YouTube, One Sec
Attention Finite

Session Boundary

Create natural stopping points within a usage session. Offer "take a break" prompts, session summaries, or "you've been here for X minutes" notices that gently close the loop.

Inverts Variable ratio reinforcement, "just one more" loops
Used by YouTube (take a break), Apple Screen Time, TikTok (partially)
Friction Intentional

Intent Verification

Before opening an addictive app or service, ask the user why they want to use it. A brief moment of reflection creates space between impulse and action.

Inverts Frictionless access, one-tap launches
Used by One Sec, ScreenZen, Intention browser extension
Friction Intentional

Cooling-Off Period

Introduce a timed delay before granting access to distracting content. "This site will be available in 10 minutes." Impulses fade; intentions persist.

Inverts Instant gratification, zero-latency access
Used by Delayed Gratification extension, Cold Turkey, Clearspace
Friction Intentional

Speed Bump

Add confirmation dialogs before consequential actions. "Are you sure?" is not hostile UX -- it's protection against the impulsive click, the accidental share, the regretted post.

Inverts One-click purchase, instant posting, frictionless sharing
Used by Gmail undo send, Twitter reply warnings, banking apps
Friction Intentional

Graduated Escalation

Start with gentle nudges, progress to firm reminders, and only reach hard blocks when softer interventions fail. Treat the user as an ally, not an adversary.

Inverts Binary on/off blocking, nuclear lockouts
Used by Opal, Freedom (session modes), Digital Wellbeing
Friction Empowering

Pre-Commitment Lock

Allow users to set restrictions on their future self while they have clarity. Schedule focus periods, set daily limits, or lock out apps in advance -- when willpower is high.

Inverts Always-available override, easy bypass
Used by Cold Turkey, Apple Focus Modes, Beeminder, Freedom
Friction Intentional

Default Reversal

Flip the default from opt-out to opt-in. Instead of needing to resist opening social media, require an active, deliberate choice to access it. Blank home screens. Empty new tabs.

Inverts Pre-selected checkboxes, auto-enrolled features, always-on feeds
Used by Blank New Tab, Olauncher, Before Launcher, Minimalist Phone
Autonomy Empowering

Algorithm Control

Give users direct control over the algorithms shaping their experience. Expose recommendation weights, let people tune their feeds, or offer chronological alternatives.

Inverts Opaque recommendation engines, engagement-maximizing feeds
Used by Twitter/X (Following tab), Bluesky (custom feeds), YouTube (topic preferences)
Autonomy Empowering

Usage Visibility

Show users clear, honest data about their usage patterns. Weekly reports, real-time counters, and ambient awareness indicators that inform without shaming.

Inverts Hidden time sinks, invisible engagement tracking
Used by Apple Screen Time, RescueTime, Digital Wellbeing, ActivityWatch
Autonomy Empowering

Time Budgeting

Let users set daily or weekly time allowances for specific apps or categories. When the budget runs out, access stops -- not as punishment, but as a boundary the user set for themselves.

Inverts Unlimited access, "lose track of time" design
Used by Apple Screen Time, Android Digital Wellbeing, Opal, Freedom
Autonomy Empowering

Notification Sovereignty

Give users granular control over what notifies them, when, and how. Let people batch, schedule, mute, or completely silence notifications per-source and per-context.

Inverts Permission-hungry apps, engagement re-activation pings
Used by Apple Focus Modes, Android Priority Mode, Hey.com screening
Autonomy Transparent

Easy Exit

Make unsubscribing, deleting accounts, and canceling services as easy as signing up. One click to leave, prominently placed -- not buried behind multiple steps.

Inverts Roach motel pattern, hidden cancellation, phone-only deletion
Used by Apple (required by App Store), GDPR-compliant services
Autonomy Empowering

Human in the Loop

For consequential AI decisions, keep a human involved. Show the reasoning, allow overrides, and never let automation silently determine outcomes that matter.

Inverts Opaque AI decisions, automated gatekeeping, black-box algorithms
Used by EU AI Act requirements, medical AI tools, content moderation appeals
Transparency Transparent

Data Daylight

Show users exactly what data is collected, why, and who has access. Not in a 40-page privacy policy -- in plain language, in context, at the moment of collection.

Inverts Opaque data harvesting, buried privacy policies, consent fatigue
Used by Apple Privacy Labels, Jumbo Privacy, DuckDuckGo
Transparency Transparent

Honest Pricing

Show the full cost upfront. No hidden fees revealed at checkout, no bait-and-switch pricing, no confusing tier structures designed to nudge toward the most expensive option.

Inverts Drip pricing, hidden fees, confusing tiers, bait-and-switch
Used by Basecamp, Pinboard, most ethical SaaS
Transparency Transparent

Right to Be Forgotten

Enable permanent, complete data deletion with easily discoverable options. When users leave, their data should leave with them -- not persist in backups indefinitely.

Inverts Data hoarding, soft-delete only, "we'll keep your data in case you come back"
Used by GDPR-compliant services, Signal, ProtonMail
Transparency Transparent

Honest Copy

Use clear, non-manipulative language in interfaces. "No thanks" instead of "No, I don't want to save money." Never shame the user for declining. Never disguise ads as content.

Inverts Confirmshaming, dark copywriting, disguised ads, manipulative CTAs
Used by Ethical design guidelines, plainlanguage.gov principles
Transparency Transparent

Visible Persuasion

When using persuasive design techniques, disclose them. If urgency is real, show the evidence. If scarcity is manufactured, don't pretend it isn't. Persuade honestly or don't persuade.

Inverts Fake countdown timers, manufactured scarcity, social proof manipulation
Used by Honest e-commerce, FTC enforcement targets
Boundaries Respectful

Contextual Silence

Match notification urgency to context. Don't buzz someone's wrist for a marketing email. Reserve high-interrupt channels (sound, vibration) for genuinely urgent communication.

Inverts Every-notification-is-urgent, engagement re-activation pings
Used by Apple Focus Modes, Android Priority Mode, Slack DND
Boundaries Respectful

Complete in Notification

Include the full message content in the notification itself. Don't force users to open the app just to read a two-sentence comment. Respect the context switch cost.

Inverts "Someone commented..." teasers, engagement-bait notifications
Used by Email clients, iMessage, Signal
Boundaries Finite

Scheduled Delivery

Batch non-urgent notifications and deliver them at scheduled times. Morning digest, evening summary -- instead of a constant drip of interruptions throughout the day.

Inverts Real-time everything, instant push for every event
Used by Hey.com, Inbox When Ready, Apple Scheduled Summary
Boundaries Respectful

Respect the Clock

Be aware of time zones and local hours. Never send non-emergency notifications at 3am. Adapt communication rhythm to the user's actual life, not the server's clock.

Inverts 24/7 notification bombardment, timezone-ignorant alerts
Used by Slack quiet hours, iOS Sleep Focus, thoughtful email tools
Boundaries Finite

Wind-Down Mode

Actively help users transition away from screens before sleep. Dim interfaces, reduce stimulation, suggest stopping, and support the biological need for a technology-free buffer.

Inverts Late-night engagement hooks, bright screens at bedtime
Used by iOS Night Shift, Android Bedtime mode, Kindle warm light
Safety Resilient

Safe Exit

Provide a way to quickly hide or exit the application for users in unsafe situations. A panic button that instantly switches to a neutral screen -- critical for domestic violence resources and sensitive services.

Inverts Sticky interfaces, "are you sure you want to leave?" prompts
Used by Domestic violence hotlines, sensitive health services
Safety Resilient

Graduated Privacy

Let users control their visibility at every level -- from fully public to completely anonymous. Provide routes to anonymity and let people reveal themselves gradually, on their own terms.

Inverts Real-name policies, forced visibility, social graph exposure
Used by Signal, Reddit, Are.na, Mastodon
Safety Resilient

Community Shield

Empower communities to protect themselves. Provide robust blocking, muting, and moderation tools that let users and communities set their own boundaries against harassment.

Inverts Platform-only moderation, slow abuse response, weaponizable features
Used by Mastodon, Discord, Block Party, Twitter community notes
Safety Resilient

Edge Case First

Design for the most vulnerable users first, not the average case. When you protect the edge case, you protect everyone. The happy path is not the whole story.

Inverts Happy path design, "most users" thinking, willful blindness to abuse
Used by Microsoft Inclusive Design, safety-first platforms
Safety Resilient

Graceful Degradation

Design technology that remains useful when it fails. No internet? Offline mode. Server down? Cached data. Battery dying? Essential features only. Failure should reduce, not destroy.

Inverts Complete failure on network loss, cloud-only everything, bricked devices
Used by Progressive web apps, Notion offline, Git (local-first)
Calm Respectful

Peripheral Awareness

Communicate information through ambient cues rather than demanding direct attention. Status lights, subtle sounds, color shifts -- technology that informs the periphery of your awareness without pulling you from your task.

Inverts Modal popups, forced attention, center-of-screen alerts
Source Calm Technology (Amber Case), Weiser & Brown
Calm Respectful

Minimum Viable Notification

Use the least intrusive channel that still communicates the message. A badge before a banner. A banner before a sound. A sound before a vibration. Technology can communicate without shouting.

Inverts Maximum-interrupt notifications, sound+vibration+banner for everything
Source Calm Technology communication patterns
Calm Respectful

Invisible Until Needed

Technology should enhance capabilities without disrupting daily life. Stay in the background until actively summoned. The best interface is one you don't notice until you need it.

Inverts Always-on dashboards, persistent UI elements, attention-seeking interfaces
Source Calm Technology, Humane by Design
Calm Respectful

Single Purpose

Build devices and tools that do one thing well. A reading device that only reads. A writing tool that only writes. Single-purpose design eliminates the temptation to drift.

Inverts Swiss-army-knife devices, feature creep, "everything apps"
Used by Kindle, reMarkable, Light Phone, Freewrite
Calm Respectful

Minimum Viable Technology

Use the least amount of technology needed to solve the problem. Not every interaction needs an app. Not every app needs AI. Strip to the essential and leave the rest.

Inverts Tech-for-tech's-sake, over-engineering, feature bloat
Source Calm Technology Principle VII (Amber Case)
Calm Respectful

Respect Social Norms

Technology should behave the way a thoughtful person would in the same context. Don't interrupt dinner. Don't shout in a library. Design with the social awareness of a good guest.

Inverts Context-ignorant alerts, socially disruptive notifications
Source Calm Technology Principle VIII (Amber Case)
Autonomy Inclusive

Accessibility as Default

Design for disabilities first -- the solutions you find will benefit everyone. Don't disable zoom, preserve font scaling, support screen readers, and let users control animations.

Inverts Accessibility as afterthought, disabled zoom, fixed font sizes
Used by GOV.UK, Apple ecosystem, WCAG-compliant services
Transparency Empowering

Data Portability

Let users export their data in standard, usable formats. Your data is yours -- not a hostage held to prevent you from leaving. Interoperability as a design value.

Inverts Walled gardens, proprietary formats, lock-in by data capture
Used by Notion, Obsidian, Mastodon (ActivityPub), GDPR exports
Friction Intentional

Accountability Partner

Support social accountability structures. Focus rooms, body-doubling, shared blocklists, public commitments. Harness social motivation for intentional use rather than compulsive use.

Inverts Isolated willpower, solo struggle against engagement machines
Used by Focusmate, Flow Club, Beeminder, StickK
Attention Empowering

Feed Elimination

Replace algorithmic feeds with intentional search or curated collections. Instead of being shown content, let users seek it. The feed is the attention trap -- remove the trap entirely.

Inverts Algorithmic feeds, recommended content, autoplay queues
Used by Are.na, Wikipedia, RSS readers, News Feed Eradicator

Essential Reading

The intellectual foundations of humane design

Calm Technology

Amber Case

Principles and patterns for non-intrusive design. The foundational text on technology that respects attention.

Stand Out of Our Light

James Williams

Freedom and resistance in the attention economy. A philosopher's case for why attention is a moral issue.

Digital Minimalism

Cal Newport

A philosophy of technology use focused on supporting the things you value, not on maximizing engagement.

How to Do Nothing

Jenny Odell

Resisting the attention economy. Attention as an act of resistance, place-based thinking, and bioregional awareness.

Indistractable

Nir Eyal

From the author of Hooked -- now the counter-playbook. Understanding triggers, making pacts, and designing your environment.

Irresistible

Adam Alter

The rise of addictive technology and the business of keeping us hooked. Understanding the mechanics to resist them.
